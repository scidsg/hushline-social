I really enjoyed working on CalyxOS when contributing with The Calyx Institute, but the death knell for de-Googled operating systems has always been distribution.

It wasn't and isn't crazy complicated to install a de-Googled OS on a compatible phone, but that's still a higher bar than literally 99.6% of smartphone users were willing to do in the name of privacy and security.

The leader in the space has been GrapheneOS, but even being Snowden's favorite OS couldn't make it break out of that 1% barrier, especially when competing with trillion-dollar organizations like Apple and Google. Then, Google updated their Android policy to make it a lot harder for small teams to keep their operating systems compatible with Pixel hardware.

In a surprise announcement (at least to me), Motorola Mobility (a Lenovo Company) announced yesterday that they'll partner with Graphene to bring their OS to the consumer market!

This is a crazy significant win for everyone in Motorola's markets. Assuming it all plays out in good-faith, this is akin to the impact of when WhatsApp integrated the Signal Messenger protocol.
